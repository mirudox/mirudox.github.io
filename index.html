The Story

Who I Am

I grew up in Mumbai surrounded by engineering. With parents in telecommunications and aviation electronics, my childhood was shaped by taking things apart and understanding how they worked. My earliest exposure to technology was not through textbooks. It was through hands-on troubleshooting with my father, configuring home networks, and calibrating high-end audio and projection systems.

When it came time for formal education, I pursued Computer Science. I learned the mechanics of software, coding, and web development. However, I quickly realized my passion was not in writing raw algorithms. My real interest was in the hardware layer and how digital systems physically interact with the real world.

Seeking a different perspective, I pursued an integrated B.Ed. and M.Ed. degree. This academic research taught me the human side of the network. I learned how people process information, how to break down highly complex technical concepts, and why effective documentation is just as important as the technology itself.

Today, these two paths have merged perfectly into Media Systems Engineering. The industry is rapidly shifting toward IT and networking. My background in computer science, my hands-on experience with AV calibration, and my focus on clear technical documentation give me a unique foundation. I am a fresher who is honest about where I am and highly intentional about where I am going.

I am not just interested in one corner of AV. Whether it is AV over IP, control systems, enterprise networking, or display calibration, my goal is to understand the complete ecosystem and build solutions that make a real impact.
