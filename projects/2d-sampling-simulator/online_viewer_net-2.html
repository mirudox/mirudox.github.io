What You're Witnessing

At 1×1, the entire photo collapses into a single average color — one "measurement" trying to represent everything. A sunset, a face, a cityscape — all become the same muddy blob.

As you increase the resolution, notice when the image becomes recognizable. That's the moment you have enough pixels to capture the variation that matters.

"The need for pixels did not come from engineering. It came from the image having variation. If there is no variation, one measurement is sufficient. If there is variation, one measurement cannot tell you where the variation occurred."

Compare a simple photo (like a solid sky) with a complex one (like a face or text). You'll see that complex images need higher resolution to become recognizable — because they have more variation that matters.
